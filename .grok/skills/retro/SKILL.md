# Facilitate a retrospective

Run a team retrospective that produces *change*, not just venting. For retro facilitation
and formats delegate to the **`sprint-expert`** subagent (and `scrum-expert` for the
canonical Scrum-Guide purpose/timebox of the Sprint Retrospective).

## Choose a format (ask, or recommend by context)
- **Start / Stop / Continue** — fast, general-purpose default.
- **4Ls** — Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed for.
- **Sailboat / Speedboat** — wind (helps), anchors (slows), rocks (risks), island (goal).
- **Mad / Sad / Glad** — surfaces sentiment.
- **DAKI** — Drop, Add, Keep, Improve.
Match the format to the team's mood and what happened this Sprint; vary it to avoid fatigue.

## The five stages (Derby & Larsen)
1. **Set the stage** — purpose, safety, a quick check-in. Norm: it's about the system, not
   blame (Prime Directive).
2. **Gather data** — what happened this Sprint (facts + feelings) via the chosen format.
3. **Generate insights** — group themes, ask "why" (root cause), find patterns.
4. **Decide what to do** — pick 1–3 high-impact improvements. Make each **SMART**, give it
   an **owner** and a check date. Consider adding one to the next Sprint Backlog.
5. **Close** — recap actions/owners, quick appreciation, and a meta "how was this retro".

## Output
A short retro record: format used, key themes, and a **table of actions** (action · owner ·
when · how we'll know it worked). Offer to save it and to carry forward last retro's actions
to check whether they actually happened (closing the loop is what kills "toothless retros").
